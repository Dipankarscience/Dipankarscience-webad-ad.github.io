Life happens, Coffee helps

By Diya NagChaudhury


An eminent philosopher once said that 'Life is an adventure that happens to us while we are busy planning for it'. While it's hard to justify how coffee helps you in sustaining through, it certainly holds good value for all work addicts out there. Ever had a chat with a coffee lover? You might have noticed that the conversation flows seamlessly and you end up having a heart-to-heart talk about things you never revealed to others. Take a break and read on to find out more.

The history of coffee dates back to the Ethiopian era where a goat herder named Haldi discovered the potential of the dark Arabica coffee beans. On eating the berries from that tree, Kaldi's goats became so full of vim and vigour that they couldn't sleep that night. From then on, the word was spread, leading to the discovery of our most cherished drink.

Coffee is known to be good for the liver. Not only does it enliven and awaken you, but it also improves brain function and lowers down the risk of diabetes and liver dysfunction. Regular consumption of coffee can even prevent Alzheimer's' disease. Decaf is a brand of coffee devoid of the caffeine content. It shares most of the health benefits of coffee.

The world's most famous coffee, the Civet coffee, can cost up to 57,755 bucks. It is popular in South-East Asia and sub-Saharan Africa. If you talk about India, the best variety of coffee comes from South India. Its popularity is such that exports of up to 80% take place for the Indian Filter Coffee. Visiting South India, stop by a coffee shop, you can get the pleasure of Granny teaching you how to make your coffee taste like freshly ground heaven.

Cassandra Clare says, 'As long as there was coffee in the world, how bad could things be?' Rushing through life, we all need to complete a full checklist of tasks. We have promises to keep and deadlines to meet. Some are managing without a good night's sleep, some are a little downcast with the monotony. A delightful cup of coffee can help you put up with all these mini-challenges of daily life in a classy way, and why not take the chance?
